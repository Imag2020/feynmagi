We selected the best ETFs that we consider the best according to our criteria:Diversified across many sectors and countries. The basic idea behind diversification is to avoid having all your eggs in one basket. This way, the impact of a single negative event is reduced. For example, if you only holds stocks from one sector and that sector experiences a downturn, the entire investment portfolio will be negatively impacted. However, if you had invested in ETF that diversifies across different industries, the impact of the downturn on the portfolio would be less severe.Low-cost. Most ETFs are already low-cost compared to active funds. But when we have the choice between several ETFs tracking the same index, we prefer a cheaper one (all other things being equal).Domiciled in Ireland or Luxembourg. Both countries have special tax treaties with many countries around the world. This makes it fiscally advantageous to invest in ETFs domiciled in Ireland or Luxembourg. You can recognise these by their ISIN code that starts with "IE" (Ireland) or "LU" (Luxembourg).To illustrate the diversification of an ETF, we provide you with the number of companies in each ETF as well as all the countries it invests in.
