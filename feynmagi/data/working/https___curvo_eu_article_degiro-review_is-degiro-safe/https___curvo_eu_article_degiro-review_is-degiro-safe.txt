Who is the regulator?As DEGIRO began in the Netherlands, they fell under the supervision of the AFM and they oversee all Dutch customers of DEGIRO. The issues identified included a lack of a clear, balanced, and adequate organizational structure. More importantly there were insufficient protection of client interests and rights, incorrect information about the separate storage of investments and cash funds, and inadequate policies on anti-money laundering, incident handling, compliance, and preventing conflicts of interestâ€‹.Some client orders were being executed by a hedge fund. Securities lending is a practice where brokers lend your stocks and ETFs to third parties. In return, the third party pays a fee, which the broker usually partially shares with you. The downside is that it exposes you to counterparty risk, in case the borrower defaults and cannot return your asset. So it can be a concern for some investors who prefer to maintain full control over their shares and who don't want to be exposed to counterparty risk.Despite DEGIRO's terms giving them the option of lending your securities, they claim they don't.
